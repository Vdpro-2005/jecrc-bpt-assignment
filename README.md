  <h2>Unit 4 — Laws, Ethics and Professional Values</h2>
  <p class="small">Covers ethical practice, autonomy, MLC, laws affecting physiotherapy, and ethics documentation.</p>

  <div class="qa">
    <h3>Section A (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)</h3>
    <ol>
      <li><strong>Two significant laws affecting physiotherapy practice:</strong> Clinical Establishment Act (2010); Persons with Disabilities Act (1995).</li>
      <li><strong>Laws pertaining to MLC:</strong> Indian Penal Code (IPC, 1860); Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC, 1973).</li>
      <li><strong>Code of ethics for equality and justice:</strong> Provide fair, equal, and non-discriminatory treatment to all patients.</li>
      <li><strong>Solidarity:</strong> Professional unity and mutual support among healthcare workers to promote teamwork and trust.</li>
      <li><strong>Ethical documentation:</strong> Accurate, truthful, confidential record-keeping maintaining integrity and legal validity.</li>
    </ol>
  </div>

  <div class="qa">
    <h3>Section B (4 × 18 = 72 Marks)</h3>

    <h4>1. Code of Ethics for Physiotherapists (18 Marks)</h4>
    <ol>
      <li><strong>Patient Rights:</strong> Obtain consent, maintain dignity and confidentiality.</li>
      <li><strong>Competence:</strong> Work within scope, continue education.</li>
      <li><strong>Integrity:</strong> Honest reporting and responsibility.</li>
      <li><strong>Justice:</strong> Equal and fair treatment.</li>
      <li><strong>Teamwork:</strong> Promote collaboration and solidarity.</li>
    </ol>
    <p>Ensures patient safety, trust, and professional respect.</p>

    <h4>2. Laws Affecting Physiotherapy Practice (18 Marks)</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Clinical Establishment Act, 2010</li>
      <li>Consumer Protection Act, 2019</li>
      <li>Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995</li>
      <li>IPC & CrPC (Medico-legal duties)</li>
      <li>Indian Contract Act, 1872</li>
    </ul>
    <p>These laws ensure safe, legal, and ethical physiotherapy practice.</p>

    <h4>3. Explain Autonomy (18 Marks)</h4>
    <p><strong>Definition:</strong> Patient’s right to make informed decisions.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Informed consent and freedom to refuse treatment.</li>
      <li>Privacy and shared decision-making.</li>
      <li>Respect for choices with professional guidance.</li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Example:</strong> If a patient refuses electrotherapy, offer alternatives respectfully.</p>

    <h4>4. Ethics Document for Physiotherapist (18 Marks)</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Defines moral, legal, and professional standards.</li>
      <li>Ensures confidentiality, consent, and honesty.</li>
      <li>Includes guidelines on record maintenance and inter-professional respect.</li>
      <li>Promotes accountability and excellence in care.</li>
    </ul>
