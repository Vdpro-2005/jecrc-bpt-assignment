Section B â€” (4 Ã— 18 = 72 marks)

1. Explain near-miss events. (18 marks)

Definition & difference: A near-miss is an occurrence that could have resulted in patient harm but was averted. Unlike adverse events, near-misses reveal latent system flaws.

Examples: wrong medication intercepted, ID mismatch detected before procedure.

Causes: human error, communication gaps, unclear protocols, workload, equipment failure.

Importance: reveals safety gaps, promotes learning without patient harm.

Prevention: standardise procedures, double checks, training, EMR safeguards, audits.

2. Explain MLC in detail (records, types, confidentiality, procedure).

MLC = Medico-Legal Case. Types: assault, RTA, poisoning, sexual assault, burns, suspicious death.

  1. Stabilise patient first.
  2. Preserve evidence (clothes, swabs, samples).
  3. Document history, findings, photos with timestamps.
  4. Notify police/MLC officer.
  5. Register MLC and maintain secure records.

Confidentiality: release info only to police/court on written request or patient consent.

3. Standardised protocols in healthcare.

Protocols are structured checklists and procedures to reduce human error. Examples: WHO surgical checklist, ISBAR handoff, central line bundle. They enforce verification and standard communication to prevent sentinel events.

4. Biomedical principles.

Autonomy, Beneficence, Non-maleficence, Justice â€” the four core ethics. Applied through consent, fairness, and safe, evidence-based physiotherapy practice.

Guides professionals to maintain standards and ensure patient safety. Covers: patient rights, competence, accountability, beneficence, justice, communication, teamwork, solidarity.
